The Experience

You do not photograph a place like this. You let it change the way you see.

These are small expeditions, never more than eight of us — twelve in Botswana. We move slowly, wait for the light, and stay long after the other vehicles have gone. I am beside you the whole time: at the viewfinder, over coffee before dawn, talking through the frame you almost made. What you carry home is not only photographs.

About

For thirty years I have made photographs in order to understand what I am looking at.

I formed myself between Parsons in Paris and the International Center of Photography in New York, and have spent the decades since with a camera between myself and the world — in the highlands of Bhutan, across the Serengeti, through the streets of Monterrey, Mexico, where I was born.

But the pictures I care about most were made standing quietly beside someone, at first light, waiting for something true to happen. I lead these expeditions because teaching is how I learn to see again. I would be glad to have you with me.

— Roberta Marroquín
